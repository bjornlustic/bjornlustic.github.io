SOCIAL MEDIA

5/17/17

Social Media... Let me start by naming a couple popular names. Facebook. Instagram. Youtube. Snapchat. Twitter. Etc.

Social media branded a generation with unauthentic virtues. Plagued by a sickness that inducess depression and a feeling of missing out, these platforms for sharing information are now doing more harm than good. They mess with my psychology. No longer can I go some place for my own pleasure. I have an urge to show off. A feeling to try and top my friends. My faithful followers. Hundreds maybe thousands of them. Waiting for me. Or so I think. I'm not that special. I'm not that different from the people who follows me. They do the same things I do. They wake up, eat, sleep, talk, text, go on the internet, and even hope that their followers like their content.

How many followers is enough. Is there a limit? Can you win social media by having the most followers? What is the ultimate goal in social media. The companies that run these websites are for profit. They have a purpose to churn out as much money as they can. The numbers are only to mess with the users psychology. Likes, followers, following, comments, pictures posted, and even ratio are all a factor in mainting a look on a social media platform. No longer are they for sharing your life with family and friends. People from all over the world want to see you. Stalk you.

In the age of information, I am the product. Advertisements know me better than I do. They know my habits. They know what I want. I can't run away. Not even an adblocker can save me. The advertisements get creative. They find loopholes on the internet to mine more data from me. But what am I doing about it? Nothing. Why am I doing nothing? I don't think I understand the dangers that are about to come from all the information I have dumped on the internet since I was 10. A decade of my life. Public. Forever. In my most unwise years.

Think... How much more data will be collected in the next 40 years?

-Bjorn
